About

I'm Freyja Feeney, a game programmer in my M1 year at Sorbonne Université in Paris and a game programming mentee at Ubisoft Paris. My work sits at the intersection of systems programming, original art direction, and story.

My games tend to have strong visual identities — I do my own illustration and pixel art — and I'm drawn to projects where mechanics and narrative reinforce each other. I also care about accessibility: I've built an audio navigation system enabling visually impaired players to explore 3D environments.

I studied at the University of Vermont, where I worked in the Morphology, Evolution & Cognition Lab under Dr. Joshua Bongard, writing my honours thesis in evolutionary computation and building physics simulations for evolving robot morphologies in PyBullet.

I've put together and led small collaborative game dev groups — including an international all-women team that made Train to Teaberry together across time zones. I'm happiest on projects with weird premises and handmade aesthetics.
